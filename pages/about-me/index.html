About me

About

My name is Marko Stamenkovic and I am 25 years old. I just finished my Master of Science in High Energy Physics at the EPFL within the LPHE group. During my studies, I had the opportunity to work on various particle physics projects. My Master thesis was focused on the search for new physics in rare charm decays. The dataset I analysed was collected by the LHCb experiment in 2016 at CERN. High energy physics is a field where a strong theoretical background mixes up with the statistical realization of the world. Analysis is enabled only through an extraordinary technological and computational effort. Naturally, I decided to pursue my adventure with a PhD and I had the chance to be accepted in the ATLAS group at Nikhef, Amsterdam, Netherlands. My work will be centered around the Higgs boson and its decay into heavy flavours.

My work so far

Compressive sensing based on optical coding - Optical realization of the Radon transform

In this work, I analyze the properties of the Spatial Light Modulators and the applicability in optical processing. Furthermore, I propose a novel mathematical formulation of the Radon transform through a Bessel function of first kind and a simple convolution. Finally, I test the sparse view reconstruction using this novel implementation.

Searching for new physics in c->u transitions

A search for new physics in rare charm decays is performed in data collected by the LHCb experiment in 2016. A blinded analysis is performed and several tools are investigated: significance optimisation in order to find the optimal requirements for the selection, multivariate data analysis through two classifiers, Boosted Decision Tree and Multilayer Perceptron, particle identification variables correction through a data-driven technique and the CLs method for the computation of the expected upper limit.

Interests
